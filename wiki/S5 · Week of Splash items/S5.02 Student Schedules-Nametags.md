### S5.02 Student Schedules/Nametags: {#s5.02-student-schedules/nametags:}

**Owner:** Directors · **Last reviewed:** 2025 board (imported from the Hitchhiker's Guide, July 2026) · **Status:** current — when you find something stale, fix it and update this line.

Below you can find the info for generating the PDF’s for Docuprint; after that, you can see a sample DocuPrint request. The cost should be in the ballpark of $530. It is super important that nothing is messed up on the request, so definitely double check it\!

**GENERATE SCHEDULES & NAMETAGS AT THE SAME TIME or there will be extra nametags/extra schedules if you cancel some registrations or whatever**

Student schedules

* On the program dashboard for your program, on the right near the bottom there is a heading “Generate Printables”. One of the quick links there is “Student Schedules.”  
* When you click the link, you’ll get a page to create arbitrary user list; this will select the list of students whose schedules get generated.  
* Choose “students who are enrolled in at least one class” and hit “Continue” to generate the pdf of schedules. (This will take \*a while\*, website is slow.)  
  * We count students as “registered” if they have signed up for at least one class, so this gets you the list of students who have done that.  
  * DO NOT choose the option of “students who have clicked on the ‘Confirm Pre-Registration’ button”; not everyone does the “confirm reg” step and so this list will not get you everyone who is eligible to come to the program.  
* Double check the schedules to make sure they look right, look at the top 2-3 of the pdf and make sure all the info at the bottom looks right; lunch items/payment/classes show up as normal, etc.

Student nametags

* Under the same “Generate Printables” header there is an option for “Nametags”.  
* I’ve never used firefox (as suggested in the “guide to happy IDs”) so if you use a different browser it should be okay, but if things look wonky then I’d suggest taking another look at those suggestions.  
* Check the option “Students” under ID Type.  
  * You can ignore the “Miscellaneous”, “Number”, and “Title” text boxes since those are just for if you’re doing blank IDs; choosing “Students” will automatically print all of the Student ones and title them “Student”  
* Make sure your program name looks correct, then click “Generate IDs”.  
* Take a quick look through the output to make sure nothing looks wonky then save as a pdf\!

On DocuPrint under “Describe your project,” write something like this and be sure to fill in all the appropriate numbers

We are looking to place a custom order for a student program. We would like 1090 single-sided black and white printed pages which are student schedules for our program. We would then like 1090 student nametags (I've attached a file showing the nametag template) printed onto cardstock. There are 6 nametags to a page, so this would be 182 pages of nametags. We would then like the cardstock nametags to be cut out/separated and stapled to the corresponding student schedule in the bottom corner (i.e. If you had a student, Jack, you would print out his schedule, print out his nametag onto a cardstock page which contains 5 other nametags, cut out his nametag, and staple it to the bottom corner of his schedule (and do that for all 1090 students)). Separate from those 1090 stapled student schedules and nametags, we would like another 1090 pages of handouts printed in black and white double sided (such that the final order would be around 1090 black and white single-sided student schedules which have cardstock nametags stapled to them and another 1090 double-sided pages). The double-sided pages will have the map on one side and the student survey on the other). Both name tags and student schedules should be in the same order (both are alphabetized, and nametags are alphabetized in stacks of 6--i.e. when you cut down all pages to get the nametags, you'll have six alphabetized stacks). That should hopefully make the cutting out/stapling more efficient. We are looking for a price estimate for how much that order would cost. The files are attached and we would be looking to pick the order up before you close on November 13th. If it affects pricing, we are a group of students and also a registered 501(c)3 non-profit. We have an organization credit card so we can pay with that when we come to pick up in the store. Thanks so much\!
