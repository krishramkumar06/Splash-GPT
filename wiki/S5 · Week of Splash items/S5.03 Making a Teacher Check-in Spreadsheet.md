### S5.03 Making a Teacher Check-in Spreadsheet {#s5.03-making-a-teacher-check-in-spreadsheet}

**Owner:** Directors · **Last reviewed:** 2025 board (imported from the Hitchhiker's Guide, July 2026) · **Status:** current — when you find something stale, fix it and update this line.

Source: In the  “How-To’s” folder  
This is the spreadsheet that will be used to check in teachers on the day of the program. There are a lot of steps to making it correctly, but it’s important that they all get done because teacher check-in can be hectic on the day of the program so doing it correctly now will save hassle and avoid mistakes later.

1. For Sprout: Make a spreadsheet with 3 sheets, one for each day of the program.  
   For Splash: Make a spreadsheet with 6 sheets, one for each block of the program.  
   For both: your spreadsheet should have the following columns: Teacher name, teacher phone number, class location, checked in?, nametag/schedule?, T-shirt?, materials/printables?, adapters, and name. You can copy the overall formatting from a past program’s spreadsheet and change the specific data, just make sure to read through the steps here to avoid repeating past mistakes.  
2. Go to this link: [https://yale.learningu.org/manage/Splash/2019\_Spring/classesbytime](https://yale.learningu.org/manage/Splash/2019_Spring/classesbytime) (also accessible via Manage\>All Printables\>Class Sections sorted by time). This will pull up all the necessary information about the classes to put in the spreadsheet.  
3. Copy the information from the sheet (teacher names, phone numbers, class location) on the website to the relevant sheet on your spreadsheet.  
   1. Alphabetize the list of teachers so they’re easier to find.  
   2. Add conditional formatting so that the row turns green when the teacher is marked as “here”  
4. Check the adapter column against [this list](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1gQH4NLumeG3TRrMMPj4dGhKUqgsnGcMv9Fgwui6aBYA/edit#gid=0), add/remove adapters as necessary.  
5. **Co-teachers:** It is important to indicate which teachers are co-teaching with someone else, as it is a much bigger deal if a solo teacher is late than if one out of three co-teachers is late. You can put all three co-teachers in the same row of the spreadsheet, or put them in separate rows and color-code the font colors, as long as it is clear to someone looking at the spreadsheet.  
6. Materials requests and printables: If a teacher has materials that we’re providing or printables that we have printed for them, that should go in the “materials/printables?” column. Check the materials spreadsheet for any Splash-provided materials and the gmail for any printables, and mark that accordingly.  
7. Mark anyone who taught the block before as “Taught block before” in the “Here?” column
