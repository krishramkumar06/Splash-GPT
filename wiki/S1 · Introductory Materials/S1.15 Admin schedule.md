Tips for making admin schedule:  
Logistical tips:  
1\.     It’s surprisingly hard to keep the “Whole day schedule” consistent with the “Individual schedule” tab. To make this easier, do **all** the individual schedules first, and then add them into the “Whole day schedule” tab  
2\.     Use Command \+ Enter to make a new line in a cell (this prevents text from overflowing and being hidden  
   
Tips for assigning tasks:

1\.     Check with Presidents if any visiting admins or past admins will be attending (you can also reach out to past admins if you think they might be willing to help out/are in New Haven; especially do this if you are low on admins on the day-of); generally, try to assign visiting/former admins to tasks with current admins so there is someone who is aware of all the up-to-date policies with them

2\.     Be sure to double check which of your admins are teaching classes to avoid conflicts; admins usually won’t put this on the “admin conflicts” spreadsheet

3\.     A maximum of two people can be assigned to each tech support position (WLH and LC) because only two people can share a Google voice number. This means that a few people will be on a lot of tech support shifts

4\.     Assign tasks (in part) based on who was in charge of what pre-Splash tasks—in other words, the person who dealt with school groups should be at school group sign in. The person who dealt with teacher material request forms should be at teacher check-in (for at least some of the day)

5\.     Reserve a few of the experienced admins to be in charge of cleaning up sign-in, setting up lunch, and cleaning up lunch, since they will (hopefully) know where things should go

6\.     Keep in mind the experience level of the admin when assigning tasks and try to assign more logistically challenging tasks to experienced admins

7\.     Only assign tech support to people who feel comfortable doing it

8\.     There should (ideally) always be at least one experienced admin in LC

9\.     With the exception of sign-in, at least one director should be at the WLH admin desk for all of the shifts. During sign-in, an experienced admin (ideally your mentor) should be at admin desk

10\.  Make sure people know that during the last two blocks, it is implied that they will help clean up no matter what they are assigned to

11\.  See the Zipcar section for details about how to assign this person tasks

12\.  If you don’t have enough admins to fill all the slots on the schedule, ask your mentor which tasks to prioritize, and which ones can have fewer people. If you have extra admins (based on the template), then either put them on clean-up duty (there should be multiple people on clean-up/float duty whenever possible), or add them to LC/Parent program or another area that doesn’t have many people. Try to make sure the people on clean-up/float duty are people who will use that time productively to help the program rather than just hanging out in Teacher lounge or going somewhere else on campus (lol we have had that happen)

   
Teacher Policies:  
   
Non-Yale Teachers: Non-Yale students are welcome to teach classes, but they must be an undergraduate or graduate student at a university somewhere (the exception to this is past admins, who we also let teach). If the person lives far away, and/or asks for travel reimbursements, then be sure to check with the Presidents if we have the budget to allow that for that specific semester. Non-Yale teachers still have to attend teacher training **if** they haven’t taught for Yale Splash before; in this case, one of the directors should schedule a FaceTime or Google Hangouts call with them and briefly run through our policies—this may sound like a pain but it should only take 10 minutes or so (you can just go over the main points).
