## S7.01 Lessons from past programs (2016–2026)

**Owner:** Directors · **Last reviewed:** July 2026 (distilled from ten years of Phils, director reports, and wrap-up notes during the 2026 Drive cleanup) · **Status:** current — add your program's durable lessons here after every Phil.

These are the lessons that appear again and again in a decade of program reflections. Sources: `Advice from Past Programs`, `Director's Report`, `End of Program Notes`, wrap-up docs and meeting agendas from 2016–2026, now in the Deep Archive. When your Phil produces a lesson that will still be true next year, it belongs on this page.

### Running the team

- **Buddy system, early.** Pair every new admin with an old admin at the *start* of the semester — for social connection and a point of reference, not just tasks. Train the old admins on how to coach first. (2023, and again nearly verbatim in earlier reports — this one keeps being relearned.)
- **Match tasks to preferences.** Keep a list of what each admin likes/hates doing ("Ashna loves A-frames, some people hate it"). Send agendas in advance so people can pre-claim tasks.
- **Directors direct during meetings.** Do director-only tasks outside meetings; in meetings, watch for people finishing tasks and re-assign immediately. Always have more tasks queued than people.
- **Never assume returning admins remember.** Cross-check everyone's work, kindly.

### Day-of operations

- **Brief in writing, days early.** The night-before briefing should be *review*, not first exposure. Circulate the written admin briefing during earlier meetings.
- **Do NOT pick up directional tape before the last class ends.** Students get lost leaving. (Learned the hard way, at least twice.)
- **Make the clean-up plan as detailed as the set-up plan** — 1–2 named people in charge, a labeled "DON'T TAKE BACK" corner in WLH for end-of-day items.
- **Door monitors float.** They should scan for teacherless classes and wandering kids, not stand still.
- **Sign-in:** stanchions and letter-split easels, volunteers at stations *before* doors open, clear exit directions to prevent bottlenecks, ~5 minutes of leeway messaging for late parents.
- **Keep the feet.** The directional footprint decals are reusable; people who walk off with them owe us $10 a pack.
- **Slack threads, not main-channel chaos.** Miscommunication is the biggest day-of issue; one thread per issue.
- **Send changes to the classroom, not the lounge.** Redirecting 50 kids overloads LC 113.
- **Chalk rules:** anything in colored chalk goes up the morning of — custodians get cranky about overnight colored chalk on WLH boards.
- **Notify Yale police** that a loud, large kids' event is happening. They remember us; keep it that way.

### Teachers

- **Grad and professional schools are the untapped teacher pool.** Email DGSs (Directors of Graduate Studies) early in the semester, every semester.
- **Don't approve single-section classes immediately** — ask for a second section or a 7–8th grade section first (middle school classes: highest demand, lowest supply).
- **No direct teacher–student email contact, ever.** Policy exists for safeguarding reasons; route everything through yalesplash@gmail.com.
- **Chase lesson plans relentlessly** — the "we don't have your lesson plan" urgent email is a semester tradition for a reason.

### Students & parents

- **Never register a student by hand**, even when parents say the website crashed. The answer is a kind, firm no — full classes stay full.
- **Tell students to make website accounts *before* registration day.** The LU website buckles under registration-morning load.
- **Students leaving early need a parent email in advance;** end-of-day departures don't need sign-out.
- **Collect student testimonials**, not just parent ones — follow up after the program while enthusiasm is high.
- **Mention accommodations in initial outreach** so families with disabilities can plan with us, not around us.

### Purchasing & vendors

- **NO CAPRESE FROM NICA'S unless "no meat" is specified** — and order half your instinct; their sandwiches are huge. (F23, in all caps in the original.)
- **Buy extra toner before printing week.** If the printer breaks mid-run, you're stuck. Mild bulk, not huge bulk.
- **Stock adapters** (Mac + HDMI) — LU's are old; teachers always need them.
- **T-shirts:** downsize the size distribution (people size down when they see the shirts); order adult sizes explicitly or people assume kids' sizes.
- **Tyco prints posters** (~$75 range historically) — finalize recruitment posters by ~week 4.
- **Record every estimated number** (pizzas, maps, brochures, lanyards, parent-lounge supplies) against actuals, in the Phil, every time.

### Facilities

- **PESTER.** If facilities/room contacts don't answer, keep emailing. No response = no buildings.
- **Recount room capacities each semester** — chairs migrate between WLH rooms; the listed capacity lies.
- **Avoid booking 17 HLH unless desperate** (far away; needed shuttles in 2023).
